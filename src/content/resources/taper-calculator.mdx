Enter your starting dose, the dose you want to land on before jumping off, and the speed you're aiming for. The calculator generates a step-by-step schedule with a chart and a table.

For **buprenorphine**, the calculator uses the established short-taper schedules from [Suboxone Rapid Taper](/mat-suboxone/suboxone-rapid-taper) where the starting dose matches a published schedule (2, 4, 6, or 8 mg). For starting doses or speeds we don't have an established schedule for, the calculator falls back to weekly percentage math.

For **SR-17**, picking "Community protocol" emits the [published 4-day descending pattern](/other-tools/sr-17) (100 → 75 → 50 → 25 → jump, scaled to your daily dose). The custom-percentage option exists for non-standard courses.

For **7-OH, MGM-15/MIT-A/DHM, pseudo, and kratom leaf**, the calculator runs weekly percentage math at the chosen speed. Community-observed dose ranges and patterns live on the compound pages linked under the form; the calculator's job is to produce a workable schedule from a percentage target, not to replace the deeper context those pages cover.

## How the math works

The percentage math is a daily cut. Each day the calculator reduces the previous day's total by the same percentage, so the absolute step gets smaller as you get lower — cutting 10% of 100 mg is 10 mg; cutting 10% of 20 mg is 2 mg. The duration presets (5 / 10 / 14 / 21 days) and the custom duration all describe the same shape: how many days to descend from your starting dose to your jump-off. The per-day percentage is derived: `pct = 1 − (jumpOff / totalStart)^(1/days)`.

As the total drops, the calculator reduces the number of doses per day proportionally (`ceil(N0 × T / T0)`) so the per-dose amount stays in a tolerable range instead of shrinking to fractions of a tablet.

For **buprenorphine specifically**, the community-validated short tapers documented on [Suboxone Rapid Taper](/mat-suboxone/suboxone-rapid-taper) are built around bupe's 28-to-37-hour half-life and the short-acting profile of the compounds people in this community are coming off. A 5-day rapid taper isn't simple percentage math; it's a hand-tuned schedule. The calculator picks the established schedule when the starting dose and duration match a published row, and falls through to percentage math otherwise.

## What the calculator won't do

- **Take into account your medical history.** Cardiovascular conditions, mental-health history, concurrent medications, and polysubstance use all change what a workable taper looks like. The calculator can't know about any of it.
- **Account for varying alkaloid content.** Kratom leaf in grams is an approximation. Concentrated 7-OH products vary in actual content per tablet. The calculator works with the number you enter; the number you enter is only as good as the product testing you have.
- **Replace a check-in person.** Most rapid tapers and community step-downs go better with someone knowing what you're doing.
- **Tell you when to abort.** If symptoms become unmanageable, the right move is to stabilize at a higher dose for a day or two and restart on a slower schedule. The [Suboxone Rapid Taper](/mat-suboxone/suboxone-rapid-taper) page covers the abort-and-stabilize discipline.

## Crisis and emergency

If you're in crisis or symptoms are unmanageable, **988** (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) and your prescriber are the right addresses. See [Crisis Hotlines](/resources/crisis-hotlines) for the full list.

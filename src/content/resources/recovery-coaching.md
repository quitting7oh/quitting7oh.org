## What recovery coaching is

A recovery coach is a trained peer, usually someone in long-term
recovery themselves, who works with you on a regular schedule by text,
call, or video to help you stay stopped. Coaching is not therapy, not
medical care, and not a taper plan. It sits next to whatever path you're
on, whether that's [buprenorphine](/mat-suboxone/suboxone-for-7oh),
[SR-17](/other-tools/sr-17), a
[leaf taper](/other-tools/quit-7-oh-with-kratom-leaf), or cold turkey
with [helper meds](/other-tools/helper-meds), and focuses on the part
those don't cover: the daily structure of getting through the first
year.

Most of what coaching provides, you can get for free. The community runs
a [Discord](https://discord.gg/quitting7oh) and
[r/quitting7oh](https://www.reddit.com/r/quitting7oh/) where people
who've been through this answer at any hour.
[Mutual-aid groups](/for-you/mutual-aid) like Kratom Anonymous and SMART
Recovery, and the [virtual meetings](/resources/meeting-schedules) on the
schedule, cost nothing. A paid coach adds what free support usually
can't: one named person who is accountable to you specifically,
structured monitoring such as drug screening, and consent-based updates
to family or a prescriber. Whether that's worth paying for is a personal
call. Trying the free options first costs nothing.

## You Are Accountable

> "Recovery doesn't happen in a clinic. It happens here."
> ([youareaccountable.com](https://www.youareaccountable.com/quitting-7oh),
> accessed 2026-06-16)

[You Are Accountable](https://www.youareaccountable.com/quitting-7oh) is a
virtual peer-coaching service based in Red Bank, New Jersey, available in
all 50 states. It pairs you with a certified peer recovery specialist for
your first year off a substance and builds monitoring and community
support around that relationship. The company is clear about what it
isn't: not a detox, not medical care, and not a substitute for your
prescriber. It's meant to run alongside a quitting path, not replace one.

**What's included.** Every plan bundles 1:1 peer coaching, at-home drug
screening, community groups, and updates to the people you choose:

| Feature | Frequency |
|---|---|
| 1:1 peer coaching sessions | 1–3× weekly, by plan |
| At-home toxicology screening | Weekly or more |
| Breathalyzer monitoring | Daily, optional |
| Recovery community groups | Included |
| Family / clinician updates | Only with your consent |

**Cost.** Self-pay starts at **$375/month** for one session a week, with
**$650** for two (the option they mark as most common) and **$850** for
three. There's a one-time **$125** setup fee if you use the breathalyzer.
Plans are month-to-month, cancel anytime, and HSA/FSA-eligible.
The service says it bills some insurance, including GEHA at no cost to
members, and is working on Medicare coverage. Confirm your own coverage
with them before you start.

**Credentials.** Coaches are certified peer recovery specialists who are
themselves in long-term recovery. The company states the program is
clinically reviewed by AJ Diaz, LMSW, that it is
[LegitScript](https://www.legitscript.com/)-certified, and that it
operates under
[42 CFR Part 2](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-42/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-2),
the federal rule protecting the confidentiality of substance-use records.

**What they report.** You Are Accountable cites internal cohort figures: 10,000+
people supported, 98% member satisfaction, an average of 9 or more months
with a coach, and roughly half as many returns to detox or rehab. These
are the company's own numbers, not independently audited. Read them as
claims, not settled outcomes.

**Scholarships.** They've mentioned a limited number of funded
spots for people coming from this community. Slots are limited and not
guaranteed. If cost is the barrier, ask them directly through
[their site](https://www.youareaccountable.com/quitting-7oh).

## Questions to ask before you sign up

Coaching is a real expense, so get clear answers before you hand over a
card. Any service that takes recovery seriously can answer these quickly.

1. **What does my plan cost, all in?** Sessions, monitoring setup, and
   anything not listed on the pricing page.
2. **Will my coach be a certified peer specialist in recovery
   themselves?** That peer match is the thing you're paying for.
3. **What gets monitored, and who sees it?** Drug screening and the
   optional breathalyzer produce results. Family and clinician updates
   are supposed to be consent-based, so confirm nothing is shared without
   your say-so.
4. **How does this fit with my prescriber and my quitting path?**
   Coaching doesn't replace [MAT](/mat-suboxone/suboxone-for-7oh),
   [SR-17](/other-tools/sr-17), or a taper. Make sure your coach supports
   the path you've chosen.
5. **What's the cancellation policy?** Month-to-month should mean you can
   stop without a penalty.
6. **Does my insurance, HSA, or FSA apply?** Get the answer in writing if
   you can.

> **Coaching is not crisis care.** A coach who answers in minutes is
> still not an emergency line. If you're in danger of harming yourself,
> call or text [988](https://988lifeline.org/). For a suspected overdose,
> call 911. To find treatment or a medically supervised detox, especially
> if other substances such as alcohol or benzodiazepines are in the mix,
> [SAMHSA's helpline](https://www.samhsa.gov/find-help/helplines/national-helpline)
> (1-800-662-4357) and [findtreatment.gov](https://findtreatment.gov/)
> are free and confidential.

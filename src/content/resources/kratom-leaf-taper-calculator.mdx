## Why leaf tapers usually end at 1–2 g/day, not zero

The community's experience with leaf is that the final drop from a low daily dose to zero is the hard part. Most self-managed leaf tapers jump off at 1–2 g/day rather than tapering all the way down. This calculator defaults to a 1 g/day jump-off; adjust based on where you actually find the cliff.

If you're tapering leaf as a bridge off concentrated [7-OH](/compounds/7-oh) or one of the [synthetics](/compounds/mit-a-dhm), the leaf section is the *back half* of the plan — you've already done the harder front-end transition from the concentrate to plain leaf, and now you're descending leaf itself.

## The math

Each day is a percentage cut from the previous day. The duration presets and the custom duration all describe the same shape: how many days to descend from your starting dose to your jump-off. An N-day taper has N entries and N − 1 cuts (day 1 = starting dose, day N = jump-off), so the per-day percentage is `pct = 1 − (jumpOff / totalStart)^(1/(N−1))`.

The calculator drops the number of doses per day as the total falls so the per-capsule (or per-toss-and-wash) amount stays in a tolerable range instead of becoming a fraction of a capsule.

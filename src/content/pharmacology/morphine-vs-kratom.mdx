The classical opioids (morphine, codeine) and the kratom-derived
alkaloids (mitragynine, [7-OH](/compounds/7-oh), [MGM-15](/compounds/mgm15), [pseudoindoxyl](/compounds/pseudo)) hit the same
receptor — but their structures, binding profiles, and what happens
when you modify them in a lab are different in ways that matter for
how dependence develops and how withdrawal behaves.

This page lines them up side-by-side: structure, key modifications,
µ-opioid receptor binding affinity, agonist behavior, and what each
modification does. It's the molecular-level counterpart to the
compound overviews in the [Compounds](/compounds) section.
